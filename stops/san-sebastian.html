San Sebastián

6 – 9 August 2026 · 4 nights
The long relaxing stay — La Concha, the world's best pintxos, fireworks and classical music.

6 – 9 August · 4 nights · drive in ~1h from Bilbao

Why it’s special

The heart of the trip. A perfect crescent beach in the middle of an elegant city, the best eating in Spain, and — by luck of timing — two festivals running at once. This is where we slow down and stay put.

About the town

La Concha bay curves between two green headlands, with the old town (Parte Vieja) tucked behind. Belle Époque grandeur, Basque soul, and a food culture that takes pintxos to an art form.

What we’ll do

  • La Concha beach — gentle, scenic, ours for four days (Freya).
  • Pintxos crawl through the Parte Vieja — the evening event for Jo and Simon.
  • Monte Igueldo — the old-fashioned funicular up to the clifftop fairground and the postcard view (Freya).
  • Aquarium on the harbour — a wet-weather and tired-legs backup (Freya).

Where we’re staying

Four nights — this is the splurge base. Book early: Semana Grande (see below) fills the city and pushes prices up. See accommodation.

On our dates — a great week for Jo

  • Quincena Musical (top-tier classical, all month). 6 Aug: Mahler Chamber Orchestra, Daniel Harding, with Daniil Trifonov on piano — our first night here.
  • Semana Grande / Aste Nagusia (8–15 Aug): the International Fireworks Competition over La Concha, every night ~22:45, free — we catch the 8th and 9th (Freya). Plus giants-and-bigheads parades, Basque music and rowing regattas.

Full detail + tickets to book: culture.

Eat & drink

The Parte Vieja is wall-to-wall pintxos bars — graze across several. Book a sit-down table for one night; the good ones go fast in August.

Practical

Old town and beach are walkable; parking is a pain, so a hotel with parking or a garage is worth it. Buy fireworks-night dinner / spot early.
