Aínsa

31 July – 3 August 2026 · 4 nights
Our Pyrenean base — a film-set medieval village and the gateway to Ordesa.

31 July – 3 August · 4 nights · drive in ~3h from Barcelona

Why it’s special

The sweet spot of the Spanish Pyrenees. You get a genuinely jaw-dropping medieval hilltop village and easy access to Ordesa y Monte Perdido — without the punishing drive times of the deeper valleys. Cooler air, big mountains, a slow start to the trip.

About the town

Aínsa sits above the meeting of two rivers in the Sobrarbe region of Huesca. Its Plaza Mayor is one of the best-preserved medieval squares in Spain — cobbles, stone arcades, a castle at one end. The new town below has the shops and amenities; the old town up top is where you’ll spend your evenings.

What we’ll do

  • Ordesa y Monte Perdido National Park — the Pradera de Ordesa valley walk is flat and family-friendly (Freya), with waterfalls and the option to turn back whenever.
  • River swimming — natural pools on the Cinca and Ara when it’s hot (Freya).
  • Wander the medieval village — castle ramparts, the Plaza Mayor, sunset over the valley.
  • One slow day — no driving, just the pool and the town.

Where we’re staying

To book — aim for just outside the old town for views and value. See accommodation.

On our dates

Quiet window between festivals — Aínsa’s castle music festival and the nearby Pirineos Sur run early–mid July, just before us. See culture.

Eat & drink

Aragonese mountain food — ternasco (lamb), local cheeses, hearty stews. Restaurants cluster around the Plaza Mayor.

Practical

Cool mountain evenings even in August — pack a layer. Park in the new town and walk up, or check if the accommodation has parking.
