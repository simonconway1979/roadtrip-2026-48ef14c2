Pamplona

4 August 2026 · 1 nights
A calm overnight in the old town — San Fermín long gone, the city ours.

4 August · 1 night · drive in ~2.5h from Aínsa

Why it’s special

The famous bull-running chaos is a July thing. Arrive in August and you get the same handsome old town — minus the crowds. A perfect one-night reset between the mountains and the coast.

About the town

The Navarrese capital wraps a tight medieval core around the Plaza del Castillo. Hemingway drank here; the citadel and city walls make for an easy evening stroll.

What we’ll do

  • Plaza del Castillo — coffee or a drink under the arcades.
  • Old town wander — the cathedral, the city walls, the route the bulls run (calmly, on foot) (Freya).
  • Ciudadela park — green space to let Freya run before dinner (Freya).

Where we’re staying

One night, central old town so we can walk everywhere. See accommodation.

On our dates

San Fermín runs 6–14 July, so we’re well clear. A normal, relaxed summer evening in the city. See culture.

Eat & drink

Navarrese pintxos around Calle Estafeta and the Plaza del Castillo. Easy first taste of what San Sebastián does at full volume.

Practical

Quick stop — drop bags, explore on foot, early-ish start next morning for Bilbao.
