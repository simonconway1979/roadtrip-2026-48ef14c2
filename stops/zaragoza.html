Zaragoza

14 August 2026 · 1 nights
One last night inland — a vast riverside basilica, tapas in El Tubo, then home.

14 August · 1 night · drive in ~4h from the Cantabria coast

Why it’s special

A real city to break the drive home, not a motorway stop. The Plaza del Pilar is one of Spain’s great squares, and a fountain on it that Freya can run through after a long day in the car.

About the town

Aragón’s capital on the Ebro. The twin-towered Basílica del Pilar dominates the riverfront and looks spectacular lit up at night. Moorish, Roman and Renaissance layers sit side by side.

What we’ll do

  • Plaza del Pilar at dusk — the basilica lit up, the walk-through fountain for Freya (Freya).
  • Basílica del Pilar — vast interior; lift up the tower for the view.
  • Aljafería palace — Moorish palace, if there’s morning time.
  • El Tubo — the tapas quarter for a final-night dinner.

Where we’re staying

One night. Book somewhere with air-con and ideally a pool — see practical. See accommodation.

On our dates

Zaragoza’s huge Fiestas del Pilar are in October, so mid-August is calm. There’s a steady summer gig calendar (Auditorio, Sala Oasis) if something lands on the 14th. See culture.

Eat & drink

Tapas in El Tubo — go early-evening when it’s cooler.

Practical

Heat warning: inland, mid-August can hit 38–40°C, and you’ll feel it after two weeks on the cool coast. Do the square at night, pick accommodation with air-con / a pool, leave by late morning for the easy final hop to Barcelona (~3.5h).
