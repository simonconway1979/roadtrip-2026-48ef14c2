Bilbao

5 August 2026 · 1 nights
One night for the Guggenheim — titanium curves and a giant flower dog.

5 August · 1 night · drive in ~1.5h from Pamplona

Why it’s special

A one-building city stop, and that building is the Guggenheim. Gehry’s titanium ship turned a faded industrial port into a design pilgrimage. Worth a night on its own.

About the town

The Guggenheim anchors a riverside that Bilbao rebuilt around it. The Casco Viejo (old town) across the river has the Seven Streets, the market, and the pintxos bars.

What we’ll do

  • Guggenheim Museum — the architecture is the headline; inside, the scale wows kids too.
  • Puppy — Jeff Koons’ 12-metre flower-covered dog out front. Freya will lose it (Freya).
  • Maman — Louise Bourgeois’ giant bronze spider by the river (Freya).
  • Casco Viejo — pintxos and a wander if there’s time.

Where we’re staying

One night, ideally walkable to the Guggenheim or the old town. See accommodation.

On our dates

Bilbao’s own big fiesta, Aste Nagusia, is 22–30 August — after we’ve left. So a clean, uncrowded museum visit. See culture.

Eat & drink

Pintxos in the Casco Viejo (Plaza Nueva), or a riverside dinner near the museum.

Practical

Book the Guggenheim slot ahead in August. Then a short, easy hop to San Sebastián the next day.
