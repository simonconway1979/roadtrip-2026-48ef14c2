Getting Around

Two short flights and three easy drives — the whole trip's travel, leg by leg.

The big decision: we fly to the coast instead of driving the loop from Barcelona. That removes about two full days behind the wheel and turns the trip into a tight cluster of short hops. Two flights, a hire car, and four short drives — only one over two hours.

Barcelona → Bilbao → San Sebastián · day one

Fly BCN → Bilbao (Vueling, ~1h20, frequent through the day), pick up the hire car at the airport, and drive an easy ~1h15 east to San Sebastián. - Stop idea: Getaria or Zarautz on the coast for a first beach stop en route. - Note: land around midday so there’s time to settle in. Child seat sorted with the hire (see transport).

San Sebastián → Ochagavía / Irati · ~2h

South into Navarre, over a mountain pass into the green Pyrenees. - Stop idea: Pamplona for lunch and a run around the Ciudadela park — it’s roughly halfway. - Note: winding mountain road for the last stretch; scenic, not a slog.

Ochagavía → Cantabria coast · ~3.5h (the one long leg)

West across the top of Spain to the eclipse coast. The trip’s only longer drive — break it up. - Stop idea: Vitoria-Gasteiz or a quick Bilbao pintxo makes a good mid-point lunch. - Note: aim to arrive on the 11th in time for the Sokolov recital in Santander that evening if we’ve got tickets.

Comillas → Bilbao (last night) → Barcelona

Drive ~1h30 east to Bilbao on the 14th for the final night, then on the 15th it’s a ~12-minute hop to the airport, drop the car, and fly home. - Stop idea: Castro Urdiales on the coast for lunch on the drive to Bilbao. - Note: book an afternoon flight on the 15th so the last Bilbao morning (Guggenheim) is usable.
