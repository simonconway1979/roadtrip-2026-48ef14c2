With Freya

The whole trip is paced for a four-year-old: three relaxed bases plus a last night in Bilbao, short hops between them, and a kid-win at every stop. Flying in means just one travel day to start and one to finish, instead of long road days.

Travel days

  • The flight is short (~1h20) — snacks, a tablet, and a favourite toy cover it.
  • Drives are mostly 1–2h; the one longer leg (Ochagavía → coast, ~3.5h) gets a Vitoria or Bilbao lunch stop to break it.
  • Her own car seat in the hire car (bring or pre-book — see transport).

A win at every stop

  • San Sebastián — La Concha beach, the Monte Igueldo funicular and fairground, the aquarium.
  • Ochagavía / Irati — flat forest walks with streams and bridges, river paddling, a stone village to explore.
  • Cantabria — beaches, the Picos cable car, the Magdalena peninsula mini-zoo.
  • Bilbao — Puppy (the giant flower dog) and Maman (the spider) at the Guggenheim, and the Artxanda funicular up for the view.

🌒 The eclipse, for a 4-year-old

  • It happens in the early evening — past the worst of the heat, but watch tiredness.
  • Her own eclipse glasses, and supervise: glasses on for the partial phases, off only during the ~1 minute of totality.
  • Make it an adventure — a picnic on the coast, then “the sky goes dark.”

Practical

  • Buggy/carrier for villages and forest walks.
  • Beach gear and serious sun protection.
  • The 3-night and 4-night bases are the rest points — build in down-days.
