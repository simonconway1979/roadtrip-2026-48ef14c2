Cantabria coast

11 – 14 August 2026 · 3 nights
Beaches, the Picos de Europa, a world-class concert — and totality on 12 August.

11 – 14 August · 3 nights · ~1h drive in from the Picos de Europa

Based at Tinas de Pechón, on the far-western coast 10 minutes from San Vicente de la Barquera — a clifftop spot with a west-facing horizon, which is exactly what the eclipse needs.

Why it’s special

The climax, and the most relaxed stretch — three nights in one place to wind down. A green, dramatic coast with open Atlantic horizons, which is exactly what the eclipse needs. Beaches, the Picos de Europa an hour inland, a serious classical festival in nearby Santander, and on the 12th the sky goes dark.

About the area

West along the coast from Santander, Comillas and San Vicente de la Barquera are small, pretty fishing towns that face open sea — better for the low eclipse sun than the city beaches. Santander itself, with the grand El Sardinero beach and the Magdalena peninsula, is an easy drive east for a day.

What we’ll do

  • 🌒 The total solar eclipse, 12 August — the whole reason we’re here on this date. Full plan: the eclipse.
  • Beaches — the calmer west-coast coves, plus El Sardinero in Santander (Freya).
  • Oyambre Natural Park — dunes and wide, wild beaches between here and Comillas (Freya).
  • Magdalena peninsula (Santander) — palace grounds, mini-zoo, sea views (Freya).

Where we’re staying — booked ✅

Tinas de Pechón — a stylish aparthotel (2-bed apartment with a kitchenette) on the Pechón peninsula, 10 minutes from San Vicente, with secure parking. Three nights, 11–14 Aug. Comillas itself sold out, so Pechón won on availability and quality — and it sits right on the eclipse coast. See accommodation.

On our dates — Festival Internacional de Santander

  • 11 Aug: Grigory Sokolov, solo piano recital (Sala Argenta, Santander) — one of the great living pianists, in town the night before the eclipse. We arrive on the 11th, so the evening recital is catchable. The cultural high point of the trip.
  • 12 Aug: chamber concert in the church at San Vicente de la Barquera — same town as the eclipse viewpoint.
  • Also: Bach Collegium Japan (10th), Dúo Elysium (13th).

Tickets: culture.

Towns to wander

  • San Vicente de la Barquera (10 min) — our local town, and one of the prettiest in Spain: a medieval bridge over the marshes, a hilltop castle and church, a working harbour, the Picos rising behind. The evening-stroll-and-seafood town.
  • Comillas (25 min) — Gaudí’s whimsical El Capricho, the Sobrellano Palace, a sandy bay and a modernist old town.
  • Santillana del Mar (35 min) — a perfectly preserved medieval village, with the Altamira museum (full-size replica cave of 20,000-year-old paintings) next door (Freya).
  • Llanes, Asturias (~35 min west) — a walled medieval port with painted harbour blocks and a string of beaches, including the hidden inland cove of Gulpiyuri.

Eat & drink

Cantabrian seafood — rabas (fried squid), anchoas from nearby Santoña, cocido montañés. The west-coast fishing towns are made for a harbour lunch.

  • A treat: Annua in San Vicente — a Michelin-starred restaurant on a platform over the estuary (confirm it’s open + reserve well ahead). Its bistro sibling Nácar by Annua is the relaxed, family-friendlier option.
  • Harbour seafood in San Vicente — Las Redes, Sotavento, Augusto (all in the Michelin Guide): super-fresh market fish, seafood and lobster/clam rice, by the water. Perfect for a family lunch.

Practical

North-coast August can cloud over — keep the eclipse plan flexible and be ready to move along the coast for a clear horizon. With the eclipse on the middle night, there’s a night either side to chase clear sky on the day. Buy eclipse glasses before the trip (checklist).
