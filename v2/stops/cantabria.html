Cantabria coast

11 – 15 August 2026 · 4 nights
Beaches, the Picos de Europa, a world-class concert — and totality on 12 August.

11 – 15 August · 4 nights · drive in ~3.5h from Ochagavía (break en route)

Base around Comillas / San Vicente de la Barquera, west of Santander — the eclipse viewpoint, prettier and cheaper than the city.

Why it’s special

The climax, and the most relaxed stretch — four nights in one place to wind down. A green, dramatic coast with open Atlantic horizons, which is exactly what the eclipse needs. Beaches, the Picos de Europa an hour inland, a serious classical festival in nearby Santander, and on the 12th the sky goes dark.

About the area

West along the coast from Santander, Comillas and San Vicente de la Barquera are small, pretty fishing towns that face open sea — better for the low eclipse sun than the city beaches. Santander itself, with the grand El Sardinero beach and the Magdalena peninsula, is an easy drive east for a day.

What we’ll do

  • 🌒 The total solar eclipse, 12 August — the whole reason we’re here on this date. Full plan: the eclipse.
  • Beaches — the calmer west-coast coves, plus El Sardinero in Santander (Freya).
  • Picos de Europa day — Fuente Dé cable car or the Covadonga lakes, an hour or so inland (Freya).
  • Magdalena peninsula — palace grounds, mini-zoo, sea views (Freya).

Where we’re staying

Four nights. Book now — the coast fills with eclipse-watchers for the 12th. A west-facing coastal spot near Comillas / San Vicente doubles as the eclipse base. See accommodation.

On our dates — Festival Internacional de Santander

  • 11 Aug: Grigory Sokolov, solo piano recital (Sala Argenta, Santander) — one of the great living pianists, in town the night before the eclipse. We arrive on the 11th, so the evening recital is catchable. The cultural high point of the trip.
  • 12 Aug: chamber concert in the church at San Vicente de la Barquera — same town as the eclipse viewpoint.
  • Also: Bach Collegium Japan (10th), Dúo Elysium (13th).

Tickets: culture.

Eat & drink

Cantabrian seafood — rabas (fried squid), anchoas from nearby Santoña, cocido montañés. The west-coast fishing towns are good for a harbour lunch.

Practical

North-coast August can cloud over — keep the eclipse plan flexible and be ready to move along the coast for a clear horizon. The four-night stay gives plenty of room to chase clear sky on the day. Buy eclipse glasses before the trip (checklist).
