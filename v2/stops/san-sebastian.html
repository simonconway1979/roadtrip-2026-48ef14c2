San Sebastián

5 – 8 August 2026 · 3 nights
The opening stay — La Concha, the world's best pintxos, and a great concert on night one.

5 – 8 August · 3 nights · arrive ~1h15 drive from Bilbao airport

Why it’s special

Where the trip starts. We land in Bilbao, drive an easy hour to the coast, and settle into an elegant city wrapped around a perfect crescent beach — with the best eating in Spain and a world-class music festival running all month.

About the town

La Concha bay curves between two green headlands, with the old town (Parte Vieja) tucked behind. Belle Époque grandeur, Basque soul, and a food culture that takes pintxos to an art form.

What we’ll do

  • La Concha beach — gentle, scenic, a few easy days to land into (Freya).
  • Pintxos crawl through the Parte Vieja — the evening event for Jo and Simon.
  • Monte Igueldo — the old-fashioned funicular up to the clifftop fairground and the postcard view (Freya).
  • Aquarium on the harbour — a wet-weather and tired-legs backup (Freya).

(Bilbao and the Guggenheim get their own night at the end of the trip, so no day-trip needed from here.)

On our dates — a great first night for Jo

  • Quincena Musical (top-tier classical, all month). 6 Aug: Mahler Chamber Orchestra, Daniel Harding, with Daniil Trifonov on piano — flying in lands us in town just in time for it. 🎟️ book ahead.
  • Daily chamber, early-music and organ side-concerts across the city.
  • Semana Grande fireworks over La Concha begin on the 8th and run nightly — we’d catch only the opening night before we move on. Full picture: culture.

Eat & drink

The Parte Vieja is wall-to-wall pintxos bars — graze across several. Book a sit-down table for one night; the good ones go fast in August.

Practical

Old town and beach are walkable; parking is a pain, so a hotel with parking or a garage is worth it, since we’ve got the hire car from day one.
