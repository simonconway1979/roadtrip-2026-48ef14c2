Bilbao

14 – 15 August 2026 · 1 nights
One last night in the city — the Guggenheim, Casco Viejo pintxos, and a 12-minute hop to the flight home.

14 – 15 August · 1 night · drive in ~1h30 from the Cantabria coast

Why we finish here

A last night in Bilbao does two things. It gives us a proper visit to a genuinely good city, and it turns getaway day into a 12-minute hop to the airport instead of a 1.5-hour drive with luggage and a tired four-year-old. The right note to end on.

About the city

Bilbao reinvented itself around the Guggenheim — a titanium river city with a handsome old town, a brilliant food scene, and green hills on every side. Compact and walkable, with a clean modern metro.

What we’ll do

  • Guggenheim Museum — the building itself, plus Puppy (the giant flower dog) and Maman (the spider) outside, which are a hit with kids and free to see (Freya).
  • Casco Viejo — the old town’s seven streets, the Ribera market, and pintxos bars for the last big graze (Jo & Simon).
  • Artxanda funicular — up the hill for sunset views over the whole city (Freya).
  • Riverside walk along the Nervión, under the swooping footbridges.

Where we’re staying

One night, central — Casco Viejo or near the Guggenheim, so everything’s on foot and the airport is a short drive in the morning. See accommodation.

Eat & drink

Bilbao does pintxos a little differently from San Sebastián — heartier, often hot. Plaza Nueva in the Casco Viejo is the classic crawl. One last proper Basque dinner.

Practical

Drop the hire car at the airport on the way out (~12 min from the centre). Book the return flight for the afternoon so this last morning is usable — Guggenheim, then home.
