Picos de Europa

8 – 11 August 2026 · 3 nights
The Picos de Europa — dramatic limestone peaks, a cable car, a mountain river swim, and medieval Potes.

8 – 11 August · 3 nights · base: Potes · ~3h15 from San Sebastián (flat coastal motorway)

Why it’s special

The mountain heart of the trip — and a dramatic one. The Picos are compact but spectacular: jagged grey limestone walls rising straight out of green valleys, peaks past 2,600 m, deep gorges. It feels properly alpine, and it’s quieter than the Pyrenees. Best of all, it sits just inland of the eclipse coast, so the drive on to Cantabria is barely an hour — no big travel day before the eclipse.

About the area

We base in Potes, a medieval stone town in the Liébana valley — bridges, a river running through, listed among Spain’s prettiest villages, and the food capital of the leg. The cable car, the river-swim spots and the dramatic walks all radiate out from here, and the eclipse coast is ~1h north.

What we’ll do

  • 🚠 Fuente Dé cable car — the standout. A 4-minute ride climbing 753 m to a top station at 1,823 m, with near-360° views of rock walls and valleys. No hiking needed; café and terrace at the top (Freya). Go early in August — queues build fast.
  • 🏊 Mountain river swim — Olla de San Vicente (or the calmer Deva river pools near Potes). A clear emerald pool with a sandy bottom; a ~40–50 min riverside walk in. Cold but magic on a hot day — bring water shoes (Freya).
  • Lagos de Covadonga — two dramatic glacial lakes ~1,000 m up; a short, near-flat loop. No swimming (protected, fined) — it’s a walk. August = shuttle bus only, so go early (Freya).
  • Pretty villages — wander Potes; the tiny stone hamlet of Mogrovejo up toward Fuente Dé; the Santo Toribio monastery. Optional: the Bulnes funicular to a car-free hamlet — a second “cool ride” for Freya.

Where we’re staying

Not booked yet — next on the list. Three nights in/near Potes — an apartment or casa rural with parking and a kitchen. Cheap up here versus the coast. See accommodation.

Eat & drink

Proper mountain food: cocido lebaniego (Potes’ signature chickpea-and-meat stew), Cabrales and Picón blue cheeses, river trout, cured chorizo and morcilla, with orujo or té del puerto after dinner. Potes is the place to eat.

Practical

  • Mornings are clearest — mist often rolls in by midday, so front-load the cable car and the views.
  • Covadonga in August is shuttle-only (cars banned): adults ~€9, kids 4–11 ~€3.50, book ahead, go early.
  • The best Asturian sights (Covadonga, the Cares gorge) are ~1h15–1h30 from Potes — cluster them into one day rather than back-and-forth.
  • Pack a fleece and a light waterproof; it’s cooler and windier up top.
