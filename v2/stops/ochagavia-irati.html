Ochagavía / Irati

8 – 11 August 2026 · 3 nights
The green western Pyrenees — the Irati beech forest, mountain rivers, and a stone village.

8 – 11 August · 3 nights · drive in ~2h from San Sebastián via Pamplona

Why it’s special

The mountain stretch, and a complete change of pace from the coast. This is the green, gentle end of the Pyrenees — cool forest, clear rivers, and stone villages — rather than the high dramatic peaks further east. After the buzz of San Sebastián it’s quiet, shady, and easy on a four-year-old.

About the area

Ochagavía is a classic Navarran mountain village of stone houses and cobbled lanes on a river, a good base with shops and places to eat. Just above it sits the Selva de Irati — one of the largest beech-fir forests in Europe — with flat, signposted walks around the Irabia reservoir, plus the wilder Roncal valley next door.

What we’ll do

  • Selva de Irati — easy, flat forest walks around the reservoir; shade, streams and bridges (Freya).
  • River swimming and paddling in the mountain rivers around Ochagavía (Freya).
  • Ochagavía village — wander the lanes, the bridge, a relaxed lunch.
  • Roncal valley — a scenic drive and the famous sheep’s-milk cheese; a gentle day out.
  • A relaxed local day — the point of this stop is to slow down between coast and eclipse.

Where we’re staying

Three nights in a casa rural or apartment — these run cheap up here. Look for parking and a kitchen, and somewhere with a garden or river nearby for Freya. See accommodation.

Eat & drink

Hearty Navarran mountain food — migas, lamb, trout from the rivers, and Roncal cheese. Simple village restaurants rather than fine dining; book ahead in August as places are small.

Practical

Mountain weather is cooler and can turn — pack a fleece and a light waterproof even in August. Roads are good but winding; the drive in over the pass is part of the fun, not a slog.
