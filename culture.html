Culture & Events

Three festivals land on our dates — fireworks over La Concha and two world-class concerts.

Heavy on the classical music Jo loves, and one of Europe’s best free fireworks shows. Three festivals fall right on the itinerary.

San Sebastián · 6–9 August

Quincena Musical (2–30 Aug)

One of Spain’s top classical festivals, running all month. - 6 Aug — Mahler Chamber Orchestra, Daniel Harding conducting, Daniil Trifonov (piano). Our first night in town. 🎟️ book ahead - Daily chamber, early-music and organ side-concerts across the city.

Semana Grande / Aste Nagusia (8–15 Aug)

  • International Fireworks Competition over La Concha — every night ~22:45, free. We catch the 8th and 9th. 🎆
  • Giants-and-bigheads parades, traditional Basque music (trikitixa, txalaparta), trainera rowing regattas in the bay, kids’ workshops.

Cantabria coast · 10–13 August

Festival Internacional de Santander (3–31 Aug)

  • 11 Aug — Grigory Sokolov, solo piano recital (Sala Argenta). One of the greatest living pianists, the night before the eclipse. 🎟️ book ahead
  • 12 Aug — chamber concert in the church at San Vicente de la Barquera (the eclipse town). The eclipse comes first that evening.
  • 10 Aug — Bach Collegium Japan, Bach St John Passion.
  • 13 Aug — Dúo Elysium, country church at Arnuero.

Just missed (for reference)

  • Pirineos Sur — world music on a floating stage over a Pyrenean lake near Aínsa — runs 9–26 July, ends about a week before we arrive.
  • Aínsa Castle music festival — also early–mid July.
  • Bilbao Aste Nagusia — 22–30 August, after we leave.

🎟️ To book early

Two ticketed concerts will sell out: Trifonov (6 Aug) and Sokolov (11 Aug). Book once accommodation is confirmed — see the checklist. Everything in Semana Grande is free.
