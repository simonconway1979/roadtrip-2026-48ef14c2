Driving & Charging

Car: BMW iX1 (fully electric). Battery ~64.7 kWh usable, WLTP ~430–475 km, DC fast charging up to ~130 kW (10–80% in roughly half an hour), CCS2 connector. AC up to 11 kW for overnight top-ups (Type 2 cable comes with the car).

Realistic range for this trip

WLTP is optimistic. Loaded with three people and luggage, at motorway speed, in August heat with the AC on, plan for ~250–280 km between charges (charging 10–80%). That makes most legs single-hop, with three that want a planned stop.

The route at a glance

All in Spain — no France, so no foreign tolls or vignette. ~1,675 km total over 7 short legs. See the drives.

Strategy

  • Overnight AC at the 4-night bases (Aínsa, San Sebastián, Cantabria) is the easy win — ask each accommodation about a charger or nearby point, so you set off full each morning.
  • Use the BMW’s built-in route planner (iDrive / BMW Charging) — it slots fast-charge stops into the nav automatically and pre-conditions the battery before arrival.
  • Back it up with A Better Route Planner — enter “BMW iX1”, it builds the exact charge stops for each leg.
  • Charging apps for Spain: Electromaps, Chargemap, plus networks Iberdrola, Endesa X, Zunder, Wenea, Ionity and Tesla Superchargers (many open to CCS cars). Carry 2–3 network apps/RFID cards as backup — coverage is good on motorways, patchier in the Pyrenees.

Car kit

  • CCS happens at the station; bring the Type 2 cable for AC/overnight charging.
  • Spain legal kit: reflective vests + warning triangles (or a V16 beacon).
  • EU driving docs, insurance, breakdown cover, EHIC/GHIC.
  • Kid stuff — see with Freya.
