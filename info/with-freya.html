With Freya

The whole itinerary was paced for a four-year-old: longer stays, no drive over four hours, and a kid-win at every stop.

Drive days

  • Keep legs short and timed around naps where possible — most are 1–2.5h.
  • The one long day (Cantabria → Zaragoza, ~4h) gets a Burgos lunch stop to break it.
  • Car kit: snacks, water, entertainment, sunshades, a comfort toy.

A win at every stop

  • Aínsa — river swimming, the castle, a flat Ordesa valley walk.
  • Pamplona — the Ciudadela park to run around.
  • Bilbao — Puppy (the giant flower dog) and Maman (the spider) at the Guggenheim.
  • San Sebastián — La Concha beach, the Monte Igueldo funicular and fairground, the aquarium.
  • Cantabria — beaches, the Picos cable car, the Magdalena peninsula mini-zoo.
  • Zaragoza — the walk-through fountain on the Plaza del Pilar.

🌒 The eclipse, for a 4-year-old

  • It happens in the early evening — past the worst of the heat, but watch tiredness.
  • Her own eclipse glasses, and supervise: glasses on for the partial phases, off only during the ~1 minute of totality.
  • Make it an adventure — a picnic on the coast, then “the sky goes dark.”

Practical

  • Buggy/carrier for towns and the valley walk.
  • Beach gear and serious sun protection.
  • Build in down-days — the 4-night bases (Aínsa, San Sebastián, Cantabria) are the rest points.
